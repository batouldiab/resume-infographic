Defragmenting the World's Knowledge — Data Chaos to Policy Action
The biggest AI challenge is not model architecture — it is data fragmentation. At UN ESCWA, Batoul processed hundreds of terabytes of fragmented human capital data from regions-in-transition, fusing Geospatial AI, Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), and Large Language Models (LLMs) to produce interactive geo-enabled dashboards that turned fragmented human capital signals into actionable policy intelligence across society, economy, and environment.

An Integrative Mind Across All Abstraction Layers — Silicon to Society
Batoul works at intersections others avoid. Her thesis fused Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs) with multi-atlas ensemble regression on functional MRI (fMRI) data to predict biological brain age beyond what either approach achieves alone. At the UN, GNNs + LLMs + Geospatial analytics forecast labour-market demand in fragile economies. As consultant: same ideas, carried from whiteboard to production MLOps and a live Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) chatbot.
